Housing options

A quick comparison of housing options is given below. We try to update the information as much as possible, but there may be discrepancies.

Fischer O'Hara Grace (FOG) Residence - Fischer Apartments
2-bedroom apartments (ON CAMPUS)
Pro:
  • Nice community
  • Students Community
  • Completely furnished
  • Very close to the campus
  • Internet and basic cable ready
  • Utilities included
  • You can get transferred to any other apartment mid-year if you need
Cons:
  • Not the cheapest option
  • You usually don't know who you are living with
Overlook Apartments
1/2/3/4 bedroom apartments
Pro:
  • Awesome location (overlooking the university campus)
  • Adjacent to the campus
  • Completely furnished
  • Very close to the campus
  • Brand new, large complex
  • Utilities included
  • Completely furnished
Cons:
  • Freakishly expensive
Clover Village
1/2 bed apartments
Pros:
  • Fairly close to the campus
  • Awfully close to grocery store
  • Mostly students community
  • Very close to the campus
  • Brand new, large complex
  • Utilities included
  • Completely furnished
Cons:
  • A little expensive
  • Not the quickest maintenance
Fischer O'Hara Grace (FOG) Residence - O'Hara Studios
4-bedroom studios
Pro:
  • All as Fischer, except that they don't have cooling
  • Cheaper than Fischer
Cons:
  • No cooling
  • 4 cooks may spoil the broth
Campus Housing (aka Notre Dame Apartments)
2-bedroom apartments
Pros:
  • Cheaper than most of other places
  • Quick maintenance
  • Internet ready
  • Option of furnished apartments
  • Safe and very close to the campus
  • You don't necessarily have to find a roommate
  • Massive size
  • City Bus stop right in front
Cons:
  • No centralized cooling/heating
  • Heaters are a little more power consuming.
Rosemary AKA Village Terre Apartments: 2 bed apartments
2-bedroom apartments
Pros:
  • Cheapest ever
  • REasonably close to the campus
  • Indian community
  • Fairly quick maintenance
  • Excellent heating/cooling
  • You don't necessarily have to find a roommate
  • Massive size
  • City Bus stop right in front
Cons:
  • Not so good if you are looking for giant size aprtments

Household Purchases

If you are looking for places to buy furniture

ND Surplus have a lot of used/extra stuff from offices and labs of the University. Most of the stuff is great quality, and some of it is brand new. The inventory is here (which keeps changing, as it is also open for general public, but you will always find something similar you see here).
Note: They don't home deliver and only accept cash/checks.
You can also visit after you come here.
University of Notre Dame also organized Old2Gold every year in May which has excellent quality furniture items at heavy discounts.
